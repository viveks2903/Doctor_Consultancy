Specialities

General Physician

Assess symptoms, perform physical examinations, order diagnostic tests (such as blood work or imaging), and provide appropriate treatment. They often manage chronic conditions like diabetes, hypertension, and respiratory illnesses.

Dermatology

Address a wide range of skin issues, including acne, eczema, psoriasis, dermatitis, fungal infections, and skin cancer. They also manage cosmetic concerns like wrinkles, pigmentation, and scars.

Psychiatry

Specialist diagnosis and treatment for various mental health conditions.
Expert support for ADHD, ASD, and other mental health needs in young individuals. Safe and evidence-based diagnosis

Pediatrics

follow-up appointments and non-emergency consultations. Specialized care for newborns. Children's Assessment Unit (CAU): A short-term observation unit for patients.

Cardiologist

encompass the diagnosis, treatment, and management of heart-related conditions. routine check-ups to specialized interventions like cardiac catheterization

Dental

Regular examinations to assess your oral health. Filling cavities and repairing damaged teeth. Educating patients about oral hygiene and preventive measures.
